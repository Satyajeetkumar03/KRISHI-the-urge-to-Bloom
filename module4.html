GOVERMENT PROVISIONS

What is Central Government Scheme?

The Central Government Scheme in terms of agriculture refers to various initiatives and programs launched by the Government of India to promote agriculture, enhance productivity, and improve the livelihoods of farmers across the country. These schemes are implemented by various departments and agencies of the government, including the Ministry of Agriculture and Farmers' Welfare, the Ministry of Rural Development, and the Ministry of Food Processing Industries.

Are Government Schemes are Necessary?

Government schemes are necessary in agriculture for a number of reasons:

  • Enhancing productivity: The government can provide farmers with various schemes such as subsidies, loans, and technical support to help them improve productivity, reduce costs, and increase profitability.
  • Ensuring food security: Governments can develop and implement schemes that aim to ensure adequate food supply for the population. These schemes may include investment in irrigation facilities, soil conservation, and the development of drought-resistant crops.
  • Promoting sustainability: Agriculture can have significant environmental impacts, and the government can provide incentives for farmers to adopt sustainable farming practices that conserve soil, water, and biodiversity.
  • Enhancing rural livelihoods: Agriculture is a key source of livelihood for millions of people in rural areas, and government schemes can provide support to ensure that farmers have access to markets, credit, and other essential services.
  • Disaster management: The government can also provide support to farmers in the event of natural disasters such as floods, droughts, or cyclones. Schemes may include crop insurance, compensation for crop loss, and rehabilitation programs for affected farmers.

Overall, government schemes are necessary in agriculture to ensure the sustainability and productivity of the sector, and to support the livelihoods of farmers and rural communities.

Central Government Schemes for farmers in India

1. Pradhan Mantri Krishi Sinchai Yojana (PMKSY)

Har Khet ko Pani "Prime Minister Krishi Sinchayee Yojana"

A scheme that aims to enhance water use efficiency, promote irrigation, and improve water management practices in agriculture.

    Vision
  • commitment toward conservation and management of water.
  • Improve the water-use efficiency.
    Mision
  • More crop per drop
  • Solution on source creation and management of water.
  • Solution about field application and activities.

Pradhan Mantri Krishi Sinchai Yojana (PMKSY) is a flagship scheme of the Government of India that was launched in July 2015. The scheme aims to enhance the physical access to water on farm and expand cultivable area under irrigation through a comprehensive approach. The main objective of the PMKSY is to provide end-to-end solutions in irrigation supply chain, viz., water sources, distribution network, farm-level applications, and efficient water use.

Under the PMKSY scheme, the government aims to achieve convergence of investments in irrigation at the field level, expand cultivable area under assured irrigation, improve on-farm water use efficiency to reduce wastage of water, enhance the adoption of precision irrigation and other water-saving technologies, enhance recharge of aquifers, and introduce sustainable water conservation practices.

    The scheme has four components:
  • Accelerated Irrigation Benefits Programme (AIBP)
  • Har Khet Ko Pani (HKKP)
  • Per Drop More Crop (PDMC)
  • Watershed Development (WD)

    • The scheme is being implemented across the country and is expected to bring about a significant change in the agricultural sector of India by providing better access to water for irrigation and enhancing the overall productivity of the sector.

2. Paramparagat Krishi Vikas Yojana (PKVY)

Har Khet ko Pani "Prime Minister Krishi Sinchayee Yojana.

An initiative launched by the NDA government in 2015.

    Vision
  • To promote organic farming
  • Use of traditional resources.
    Mision
  • Farmers are encouraged to form clusters or groups.
  • The aim is to form 10,000 clusters over the coming years.
  • To bring five lakh acres of agricultural land under organic farming.
  • Certification costs are to be borne by the government.
  • Each cluster or group has 50 farmers willing to take up organic farming.
  • Each farmer who enrolls in the scheme is to be provided Rs 20000 per acre.

Paramparagat Krishi Vikas Yojana (PKVY) is a scheme launched by the Government of India in 2015, which aims to promote organic farming among small and marginal farmers. The scheme is implemented by the Ministry of Agriculture and Farmers Welfare and is being implemented across the country.

The main objective of the PKVY scheme is to promote eco-friendly and sustainable agricultural practices that are in sync with nature, reduce dependence on chemical fertilizers and pesticides, and improve soil health. The scheme also aims to promote the production of organic crops that are in high demand both in the domestic and international markets, thereby increasing the income of farmers.

Under the PKVY scheme, financial assistance is provided to farmers for setting up of organic farming clusters of 50 acres each. Farmers are encouraged to form groups or clusters and take up organic farming under the guidance of technical experts.he scheme also provides assistance for the purchase of bio-fertilizers, vermicompost, green manure, and other organic inputs.

The scheme promotes the use of traditional and indigenous knowledge of farming, which has been passed down through generations. The scheme also seeks to ensure that the entire value chain of organic produce, from production to marketing, is streamlined and effective.Overall, the PKVY scheme is aimed at promoting sustainable agriculture practices, improving soil health, reducing the cost of cultivation, and increasing the income of farmers.

3. Pradhan Mantri Fasal Bima Yojana (PMFBY)

It is a crop insurance scheme where multiple stakeholders are integrated on a single platform.

    Vision
  • To provide the farmers a stable source of income.
  • Farmers need not worry about income during natural calamities.
    Mision
  • Farmers to be given financial support by way of insurance.
  • Farmers continue to do farming even when faced with crop loss.
  • Make the farmers self-reliant.
  • Credit flow is ensured in case of crop loss due to pests and natural calamities.

Pradhan Mantri Fasal Bima Yojana (PMFBY) is a crop insurance scheme launched by the Government of India in 2016. The scheme aims to provide insurance coverage to farmers against crop loss due to natural calamities, pests and diseases, and other unforeseen events that affect the yield of crops.

Under the PMFBY scheme, farmers pay a premium of only 2% for Kharif crops, 1.5% for Rabi crops, and 5% for horticultural crops. The rest of the premium is paid by the government, which makes it an affordable and accessible scheme for farmers.

The scheme covers all food crops, oilseeds, and commercial crops. The insurance coverage is provided from the sowing stage to the post-harvest stage. In case of crop loss, farmers are compensated for the loss of yield at the market rate.

The scheme also aims to encourage the use of modern technology and good agricultural practices to improve crop productivity and reduce crop loss. The scheme provides a higher insurance cover for crops that are grown using good agricultural practices, thereby incentivizing farmers to adopt such practices.

The PMFBY scheme is being implemented across the country through the participation of various stakeholders, including insurance companies, banks, and state governments. The scheme has provided relief to farmers in times of crop loss due to natural calamities and has helped in reducing the agrarian distress in the country.

4. Gramin Bhandaran Yojana

Under this, scheme the government aims to provide storage facilities to the farmer in rural areas.

    Vision
  • Create modern storage facilities in rural areas.
  • Farmers are encouraged to maximize their output.
    Mision
  • Creating enough storage facilities.
  • Farmers can store farm produce, processed farm produce, and agricultural inputs.
  • Improve the marketability of farm produce.
  • Prevent the sale of produce immediately after harvest due to distress.

Gramin Bhandaran Yojana (GBY) is a scheme launched by the Government of India in 2001 to provide agricultural storage facilities to farmers in rural areas. The scheme aims to promote scientific storage of farm produce, reduce post-harvest losses, and improve the income of farmers.

Under the GBY scheme, financial assistance is provided to farmers for the construction of godowns, storage facilities, and other post-harvest infrastructure in rural areas. The scheme also provides assistance for the renovation and expansion of existing storage facilities.

The scheme is implemented by the National Bank for Agriculture and Rural Development (NABARD) in collaboration with the State Governments. The assistance provided under the scheme is in the form of subsidy or grant, depending on the category of the beneficiary.

The GBY scheme has several benefits for farmers. It helps in reducing post-harvest losses by providing proper storage facilities, thereby ensuring better prices for the produce. The scheme also helps in improving the quality of the produce by providing a suitable storage environment. The scheme also creates employment opportunities in rural areas by generating demand for construction and maintenance of storage facilities.

Overall, the GBY scheme is aimed at providing storage facilities to farmers in rural areas and promoting scientific storage of farm produce. The scheme has been successful in reducing post-harvest losses, improving the income of farmers, and creating employment opportunities in rural areas.

5. Pradhan Mantri Kisan Maan-Dhan Yojana (PM-KMY)

This scheme is especially for farmers between the age of 18-40 years. To avail of the benefit of this scheme, the farmer has to register
under the pension fund.

    Vision
  • The Welfare of small and marginal farmers
  • Provide monthly income to farmers above 60 years of age
    Mision
  • To provide a minimum monthly income of Rupees 3000 for farmers after the age of 60.
  • The Scheme is administered through the farmers’ cooperation and welfare department of agriculture.

Pradhan Mantri Kisan Maan-Dhan Yojana (PM-KMY) is a pension scheme launched by the Government of India in 2019 for small and marginal farmers. The scheme aims to provide social security to farmers in their old age by providing them with a pension of Rs. 3000 per month after the age of 60. Under the PM-KMY scheme, farmers between the ages of 18 and 40 can enroll themselves by paying a nominal monthly contribution. The Central Government provides an equal matching contribution to the pension fund. The beneficiary farmer is required to contribute a monthly amount ranging from Rs. 55 to Rs. 200, depending on their age of entry.

The scheme also provides for an insurance cover of Rs. 2 lakh in case of accidental death or disability of the enrolled farmer. The premium for the insurance cover is borne by the government. The scheme aims to provide social security to farmers who are often left out of the formal pension system due to the irregular nature of their income. The scheme also aims to address the agrarian distress in the country and improve the economic well-being of farmers.

The PM-KMY scheme is being implemented by the Ministry of Agriculture and Farmers Welfare in collaboration with the Life Insurance Corporation of India (LIC). The scheme has been successful in enrolling a large number of farmers, and the government has made efforts to raise awareness about the scheme in rural areas. Overall, the PM-KMY scheme is aimed at providing social security to small and marginal farmers in their old age and improving their economic well-being. The scheme has been successful in enrolling a large number of farmers and providing them with a sense of financial security.

6. PM Kisan Samman Nidhi Yojana

The main objective of the scheme is to transfer an amount of Rupees 6000 to the account of farmers annually. This scheme is very helpful to all those small and marginal farmers who need to buy various farm implements and meet other agricultural expenses.

    Vision
  • The scheme is intended to make the small and marginal farmers strong.
  • The small and marginal farmer is in a position to sustain his activities.
  • To make the farmer attentive and active.
    Mision
  • Income support to all land-holding farmers.
  • Ensure the sustainable growth of small and marginal farmers.
  • Small farmers can buy various farm implements.

Pradhan Mantri Kisan Samman Nidhi Yojana (PM-Kisan) is a central government scheme launched in 2019 to provide financial assistance to small and marginal farmers in India. The scheme aims to provide income support to farmers by providing them with direct cash transfers. Under the PM-Kisan scheme, eligible farmers receive Rs. 6000 per year in three equal installments of Rs. 2000 each. The scheme is open to all farmers, including sharecroppers and tenant farmers. The scheme does not have any exclusion criteria based on land holding or crop types, making it more inclusive.

The scheme is fully funded by the Government of India and the benefits are transferred directly into the bank accounts of the beneficiaries. The scheme has an online portal where farmers can register themselves and check their eligibility status. The PM-Kisan scheme has several benefits for farmers. It provides financial support to farmers to meet their farm expenses and improve their livelihood. It also helps in reducing agrarian distress by providing income support to farmers. The scheme has helped in improving the economic condition of farmers, especially those in the low-income category.

The PM-Kisan scheme is being implemented by the Ministry of Agriculture and Farmers Welfare, and has been successful in reaching a large number of farmers across the country. As of March 2023, over 15 crore farmers have been enrolled under the scheme, and the government has disbursed over Rs. 1 lakh crore in benefits. Overall, the PM-Kisan scheme is aimed at providing financial assistance to small and marginal farmers in India and improving their livelihood. The scheme has been successful in reaching a large number of farmers and providing them with direct cash transfers, thereby improving their economic well-being.
